How knowledgr will change the publishing sector

A technical and strategic overview of our decentralized knowledge marketplace.

1. Abstract

knowledgr is a decentralized marketplace for digital knowledge assets — primarily ebooks and academic works — built on the Ethereum blockchain. Our platform eliminates intermediaries, ensures permanent content storage via Arweave, and gives creators full control over their intellectual property through smart contract-enforced royalties.

2. The Problem

Traditional digital publishing platforms capture 30–70% of creator revenue. Content can be removed without notice. Buyers never truly own their purchases — a license can be revoked at any time. Authors have no transparent mechanism to verify sales or enforce rights globally.

3. Our Solution

Each knowledge asset on knowledgr is minted as an NFT on Ethereum. The content itself is stored permanently on Arweave — a decentralized protocol designed for permanent data storage. Smart contracts automatically distribute royalties to creators with every secondary sale, with no human intermediary.

4. Tokenomics

The platform features a native governance token (KNWL) which holders use to vote on platform upgrades, fee structures, and content moderation policies. Liquidity providers and active creators are rewarded with staking yields from platform fees.

5. Smart Contract Architecture

All contracts are open source and audited by third-party security firms. The marketplace contract handles listing, bidding, and instant settlement. The royalty splitter contract ensures creators receive their percentage on every resale. IP registration uses ERC-721 tokens with metadata stored on IPFS.

6. Roadmap Summary

  • Q4/2023 — Concept, architecture design, partner outreach
  • Q1/2024 — Beta launch, core marketplace, early adopter program
  • Q2/2024 — iOS and Android mobile applications
  • Q3/2024 — DAO governance, KNWL token, community voting

7. Conclusion

knowledgr represents a fundamental shift in how knowledge is owned, distributed, and monetized. By combining Ethereum's trust layer with Arweave's permanence and a community-governed DAO, we are building the infrastructure for the next era of digital publishing — one where creators and readers benefit equitably.
